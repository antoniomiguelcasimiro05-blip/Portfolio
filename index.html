Digital Task 5 – Calculus in Motion

In this task, I explored how calculus describes motion and change in real life. Instead of just solving equations, it helps explain how things move, like cars speeding up or slowing down.

Velocity tells how fast something is moving, while acceleration shows how that speed changes. For example, when a car speeds up, its acceleration is positive, and when it slows down, it becomes negative.

If we use a function like s(t) = t², we can find velocity by getting the derivative: v(t) = 2t. This means the speed increases over time. Taking another derivative gives a(t) = 2, showing constant acceleration.

This applies to real life because machines, vehicles, and even rides depend on smooth motion. Engineers use calculus to make sure everything works safely and efficiently.

Reflection

This helped me understand that calculus is really about change happening over time. It made the lesson feel more practical because I can relate it to real situations, not just math problems.
